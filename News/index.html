Subject: Heads-Up on QR-Code and CAPTCHA-Gated Phishing 
Hi team,

Microsoft has just published its Q1 2026 email threat report, and two trends are worth a quick heads-up.

QR-code phishing is up 146% over the prior quarter, and 70% of these messages now arrive as PDF attachments rather than direct links. The QR code is the lure -- scanning it on a phone often takes you outside your work device's protections to a fake sign-in page.

CAPTCHA-gated phishing is also up sharply (125%). These messages send you to a real-looking "prove you are human" challenge page first, which is designed to slip past automated scanners and convince you the destination is legitimate. After the CAPTCHA, you are sent to a fake Microsoft, DocuSign, or Adobe sign-in.

What to do:
- Do not scan QR codes inside emails or PDF attachments unless you were expecting them.
- Treat any "complete this CAPTCHA to view your document" prompt as suspicious.
- If a message asks you to sign in to Microsoft, Adobe, DocuSign, or similar, navigate to the service directly in your browser instead of clicking the link.

When in doubt, forward the message to me and I will take a look.

Thanks,
Larry
