Client Alert Templates

Subject: Heads-Up: Fake IT Support Messages on Microsoft Teams 
Hi team,

We are seeing an active campaign where attackers first flood a user's inbox with junk email, then follow up over Microsoft Teams pretending to be IT support and offering to "fix" the inbox. They walk users to a fake mailbox-repair page that captures the user's credentials.

Here is what to watch for:
- An unexpected Teams message from someone claiming to be IT support, often right after a sudden surge of junk email
- A request to share your screen, allow remote access, or run a "Mailbox Repair" or "Quick Assist" tool
- Pressure to act immediately to resolve a "security issue" or "mailbox problem"
- Being asked to re-enter your Microsoft password on a page that looks like a Microsoft sign-in

Our team will never contact you unsolicited via Teams to request remote access or ask you to re-enter your password. If you receive a Teams message like this, decline, do not click any links, and contact us directly to verify.

Thanks,
Larry
