Client Alert Templates

Subject: Security Alert: Watch for Fake IT Support Requests via Microsoft Teams 
Hi team,

We want to flag an active attack campaign where criminals impersonate IT helpdesk staff through Microsoft Teams. These attacks often start by flooding your inbox with junk email, then following up via Teams offering to "help" — which is how they gain remote access.

Here is what to watch for:
• An unexpected Teams message from someone claiming to be IT support
• A request to share your screen or allow remote access through Quick Assist or another tool
• Urgency or pressure to act immediately to resolve a "security incident"
• Being asked to approve sign-in prompts or disable security settings you did not initiate

Our team will never contact you unsolicited through Teams to request remote access. If you receive a Teams message from someone claiming to be from IT support that you did not request, decline and contact us directly to verify.

Thanks,
Larry

Microsoft Teams Helpdesk Impersonation Campaign Delivers SNOWBELT/SNOWGLAZE Malware (UNC6692) Act Today Score: 10/11

Attackers attributed to UNC6692 are impersonating IT helpdesk staff via Microsoft Teams to deliver a multi-stage malware suite including browser extension SNOWBELT, Python tunneler SNOWGLAZE, and persistent backdoor SNOWBASIN. The attack begins with email bombing to overwhelm the victim’s inbox, followed by a Teams message offering assistance and requesting remote access via Quick Assist. Organizations using Teams with external tenant access should review cross-tenant communication policies and restrict or audit external Teams messaging.
